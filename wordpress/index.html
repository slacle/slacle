I first started making websites with WordPress back in 2009. I have made, worked on, and maintained many websites with WordPress since then. I have attended WordPress meetups in different countries and was one of the organizers of the Medellin WordPress Community.

WordPress is a Content Management System which allows the owner of the website to add, delete, and change content. Content means pages or posts with their text and images, but not how this content is displayed or how the website works. That is the job of the theme. The theme is what decides how the content is displayed and laid out, and how the site behaves in general. When making a website with WordPress, basically what is being made is the theme.

A theme can be made in two ways. By creating the theme directly with code, or by using a theme builder such as Elementor. There are pros and cons of working with each and it depends on the needs of each project whether one or the other is better suited for the job.

There are free and premium paid themes and theme builder templates to be found all over the internet which can be used as is or as a starting point to then customize further. For a more specialized design, a theme can be made from scratch.
